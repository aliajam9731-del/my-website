Let’s Walk This Journey Together

Hi, I’m Ali—24, endlessly curious about life, and stubbornly chasing big dreams, even when the path ahead is fuzzy. This site isn’t a brag reel. It’s a glimpse into my world: a postcard from someone still figuring it out, with grand goals and nagging questions. Like, do I focus on ending extreme poverty here on Earth, or propelling humanity to the stars? Why choose? True progress means tackling both—feeding the hungry while reaching for the Moon, building clean water systems alongside rocket ships, teaching kids to read and dreaming of raising my daughter... maybe up there. I cut my teeth at Sharif University of Technology—the “get in hard, get out harder” kind of place that shaped me deeply. But that’s just one chapter. From cave fires to AI, I celebrate how far we’ve come (even if we sometimes forget). Here, I share where I’m at, what’s on my mind, and an open invite: Let’s explore, grow, and connect—together.

Mission & Dreams

Building wealth and freedom

I want to become a millionaire—not just for the money itself, but for the freedom and power it brings. Freedom to make choices, to create, and to live life on my terms. Having my own business is a big part of this—a place where I can build, experiment, and grow.

A personal project: seeing the world

Travel is education—truly understanding this planet and its people. This journey helps me learn and grow beyond books and screens.

Lifting one million people out of extreme poverty

Help at least one million people living on less than two dollars a day escape poverty—vaccinated, educated, well-fed, and with dignity—before I turn 40.

Studying at Harvard

Learn with some of the brightest minds on the planet. Work together to make the world better—not just dream about it, but actually do it.

Witnessing Humanity's Dawn on the Moon

Be part of humanity’s next leap. A symbol of hope, progress, and the future we’re building beyond Earth.

Always a Student

For me, learning isn’t just something you do in school—it’s life itself. I’m obsessed with growing, questioning, and figuring things out. Learning is how I stay alive—mentally, emotionally, spiritually.

I studied mechanical engineering at Sharif University of Technology, known as the toughest and most competitive university in Iran. Getting in was hard. Getting out was harder. But learning didn’t stop there. It won’t stop anywhere.

I read books. I listen to people smarter than me. I test ideas in real life—sometimes they work, sometimes they crash. Both teach me something. Right now, my learning path is a mix of business, technology, psychology, social impact, and life itself. If I’m standing still, something’s wrong. Growth is the point.

Notes from Me

I love reading—but honestly, I love writing just as much. I’ve built mental models about how the world works… or how I wish it worked. Maybe it’s engineering, maybe just my wiring—but I’m obsessed with making things make sense: breaking down ideas, organizing thoughts, creating systems out of chaos. Sometimes these turn into diagrams. Sometimes into math. Don’t panic—I grew up with math. It’s a comfort thing.

I don’t think I’m a great engineer, but I did learn one valuable skill: thinking in structures. Asking: “How does this really work?” That’s what you’ll find here—ideas, frameworks, and personal notes, my way of understanding life one model at a time.
